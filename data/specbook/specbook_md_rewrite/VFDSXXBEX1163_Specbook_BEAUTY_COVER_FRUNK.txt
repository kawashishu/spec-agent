**3.7.1 Test Plan:**  
The test plan specifies by whom, when, and which devices will be utilized in the testing process. It must indicate results in a visually clear manner and the testing progress consistently. Controlling the test results must be ensured at all times.  

B2P SUP will conduct contracted tests, while test procedures and documentation require approval from VF’s validation engineer and component lead engineer. The test plan must be supplemented or updated weekly, or as new development data emerges, reflecting changes.  

The B2P SUP must ensure timely transmission of all relevant test protocols/reports to VinFast and permit VinFast's presence at testing locations. Approved testing procedure deviations must be agreed upon between VinFast and B2P SUP.  

If tests were performed incorrectly due to wrong parts, missing items, or altered procedures, they must be repeated by B2P SUP at their expense. For negative test outcomes, re-testing must be planned and financed by B2P SUP, assuming responsibility for the failure.

- Number of tested components/parts.

3.7.3 Testing.
3.7.3.1 General.
The B2P SUP provides in time a detailed testing plan in consideration of the vehicle timing chart. It must be guaranteed that the worst-case component/system variant will be tested. In order to prevent double testing, the mechanical and electrical tests have to be combined as far as possible.

3.7.3.2 Release by test-departments.
The responsible VF test department will release the components only if the agreed test targets are achieved from the B2P SUP and during the complete vehicle integration tests. The B2P SUP must submit positive test reports of all required tests according to DVP&R. In case of any problems (negative test results, wrong parameters/processes, etc.), the B2P SUP must submit a test report, must be present upon request, and deliver proposals for solutions (to keep all milestones, to observe all targets...). OK or NOK result needs to be stated on the first sheet of the report.

3.7.3.3 Analyses, test-reports.
The B2P SUP has to provide measurement reports (VFDST00008900) in electronic format with agreed alignments and measurement concepts for all parts during the prototype generations. After individual tests, the test samples have to be analyzed in detail.

The measurement and test results are to be proven by means of individual values and documented in a test report. The report has to be handed over to the responsible VF department for release. Upon request, the results together with the documentation of the tests carried out (initial sample inspections and inspections carried out parallel to development) are to be submitted to the specialist department responsible for release.

Tests carried out parallel to series production are to be planned and documented at regular intervals or as required.

- Testing with an exact naming of testing standard, norm, legal requirement, or exact description of the agreed testing procedure.

### 3.7.5 Documentation of result

- A short description of the result should include pictures, tables, detailed results, photo documentation, comparisons with earlier test reports, evaluations of results (OK/NOK), and pictures of the parts in testing positions.
- A comparison must be made with results from the specification book, testing specifications, and legal requirements.
- Recommendations should be included.
- Descriptions of corrective measures or further approaches are required in cases of negative test results.
- A progressional testing status should be documented.
- A listing of all tests should be compiled in a table with responsible persons, part statuses, and comparisons of target dates versus due dates. For negative test results, the B2P SUP should propose a new date for re-testing with countermeasures, subject to approval by VinFast, including identification of the responsible person and company conducting the test.

The VF test engineer must be informed within two working days of any negative test results. The engineer should also receive updates periodically on the actual status and progress of tests. If VinFast orders parts for testing, a detailed description of the parts must be included.

### 3.7.6 Archival storage of tested parts

Testing parts may only be scrapped if VinFast's test department explicitly agrees. Upon request from the test engineer, complete testing documentation (reports, parts, and descriptions) must be provided.

### 3.7.7 Virtual testing

- The B2P SUP must use suitable calculation methods and create adequately detailed calculation models to secure, verify, and optimize the component properties defined in the technical specifications, test procedures, and legal regulations.
- For mechanical properties such as rigidity, strength, and dimensional stability under heat and passive safety, Finite Element Method (FEM) calculations should be employed.
- Tool-dimensioning for injection molding must be optimized through corresponding simulations.
- Upon request, operative calculation models representing respective development statuses must be made available to relevant technical departments of VinFast for internal calculations and integration work.
- The B2P SUP must provide FEM models to VinFast without additional cost if requested.
- Testing results must be documented as calculated (e.g., using FEM) before tool dimensioning.

### 3.7.8 Component testing

The detailed required testing is outlined in the DVP (Design Validation Plan). The DVP must be agreed upon and binding between VF, VinFast, and the B2P SUP.

### 3.7.9 Mechanical integration tests

Mechanical integration tests will be conducted in the context of a complete vehicle.

3.7.9 Mechanical integration tests  
- Climatic change test (VFDST00046100).  
- Sunlight simulation (VFDST00053800).  
- Mounting/dismounting (VFDST00009000).  
- Vibration combined with temperature (VFDST00062600).  

Basically, no distortion and no functional disturbance on the whole functional temperature range according to VTS is allowed. Particularly when materials with high and varying thermal expansion coefficients are used, the behavior must be maintained. Tolerances for flush and gap plans after climatic tests and sun simulations must not be exceeded.

4.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements  
4.3.1 North America  
a. Homologation and Safety Regulation Activities  
The supplier must ensure that their parts, products, or systems comply with applicable regulations, including European standards, Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS).
Page 13
The supplier must provide and/or support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.

**4.3.1 North America**

**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities.**  
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., European, Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS).

**b. Self-certification and test report(s).**  
The supplier is responsible for “self-certifying” their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain FMVSS/CMVSS. The test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  
The supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) that USA/Canada Governments use to evaluate the compliance of their products with each FMVSS/CMVSS.  
(*) Refer to Appendix 8 of Manufacturer Handbook.

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations.**  
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.  
The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of non-compliance or a safety-related defect.  
The supplier must propose an appropriate risk assessment and resolution plan.

**4.3.2 Europe**

**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities.**  
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.

**b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s).**  
The supplier is responsible for getting their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain UN-ECE at authorized technical service(s). The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  
VinFast also requests suppliers to provide supporting documentation, technical information, including drawings to be submitted to the relevant authorities in order to obtain certification for the EC vehicle type-approval (if any).  
The supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures that European authorities use to evaluate product compliance with each corresponding UN-ECE regulation.

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of non-compliance with UN-ECE Regulations.**  
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.
