## Technical Requirements  

### General Design and Functional Standards  
- The system/component must maintain full functionality for at least 10 years or 160,000 km, whichever comes first.
- It should be designed for high-volume production.
- Components must remain maintenance-free for the vehicle's lifetime.
- They should emit no disturbing noises.
- Must ensure full functionality for at least 30,000 open-close hood cycles.
- Standard and common parts should be used wherever possible and reasonable.

### Certification Requirements:  
- Certification tests must simulate conditions equivalent to 10 years of exposure or 160,000 km of customer service.
- Suppliers should conduct simulated durability tests to validate compliance with VinFast requirements and provide corresponding test reports.
- VinFast oversees vehicle-level integration and certification programs, with suppliers required to respond promptly to failure reports.
- Suppliers must conduct and complete all tests for their components/subsystems.
- Certification may be carried out by the supplier or authorized third parties.
- Suppliers are obligated to complete all specified testing and report results accurately.
- Test certification plans must outline sample quantities, test sequences, timing, and locations, approved by VinFast engineers.

### General Description of the System/Component:  
- Compliance with all requirements in the technical specifications must be audit-ready by VinFast.
- Offers related to sub-suppliers must be aligned with operational purchasing agreements.
- Development processes must meet system-specific requirements and fulfill laws/regulations of target markets without modifications to rating assessments.
- Technical proposals exceeding the planned development scope should identify impacts to cost, quality, and schedules.

### Passive Safety Requirements:  
- All implemented safety measures must adhere to legal standards for the target markets.
- Adjustments should ensure compliance with updates to ratings or protocols governing homologation deadlines.

3.2.1.1 Requirements.
- The parts shall be consistent not only with the drawings and specifications listed in this document, but also with any additional requirements in this document. Any future changes to the drawings, specifications, or SOR shall also be carried out in accordance with the revised requirements.
- The supplier shall decide the material selection according to the product design, durability, and performance requirements. The supplier shall provide the material details and physical properties of each part, which shall be confirmed by VINFAST.
- The parts and components shall meet the technical requirements for surface treatment in the drawings, and there shall be no bubbles, damages, side defects, or other defects. The outer surface of the assembly shall be free from obvious bruises, bumps, haircuts, and burrs, and the coating shall be free from defects such as cracks, peeling, hanging, and exposure.
- The supplier must meet the quality commitment made to VINFAST, that is, for each part sent to party a, 10 waste products per 1,000,000 pieces must be guaranteed. Important features: CPK 1.33.
- Components performance requirements shall include electrical performance, limit life test, malicious test, limit condition test, upper and lower limit deviation test, durability test, corrosion test, and other special component performance tests developed by VINFAST.
- Confirm that the parts validation test shall be carried out in a laboratory meeting the national qualification requirements.
- Interior components shall meet VINFAST's VOC limit and odor rating requirements for interior components assembly.
- Metal parts and systems shall meet the technical requirements for anti-corrosion of complete vehicles and parts.

3.2.1.2 Technical Requirements.
- The assembly shall be manufactured in accordance with the drawings and technical documents approved by the specified procedures in accordance with the ISO 19642-1:2019(en) "automobile control cable assembly" standard.
- Cable assembly should be able to work properly in an ambient temperature range of -40℃ to 75℃.
- No metal parts are allowed to have cracks, bruises, corrosion, deformation, flash, burrs, or other defects that affect performance.
- Non-metallic parts shall have uniform, smooth color, and no bubbles, cracks, lack of materials, or other defects.
- The core wire shall be coated with grease, and the performance of the grease shall comply with ISO 12924:2010(en).
- Cable assembly load efficiency should not be less than 65%, and the sample load efficiency should not be less than 70%.

### 4.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements:

#### 4.3.1 North America:  
**a. Homologation and Safety Regulation Activities:**  
- The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., European, Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS).  
- The supplier must provide and/or support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.

**b. Self-Certification and Test Report(s):**  
- The supplier is responsible for “self-certifying” their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before those products can be offered for sale.  
- VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain of the FMVSS/CMVSS. The test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.
Page 13
The Supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) that USA/Canada Governments use to evaluate the compliance of their products with each FMVSS/CMVSS.  
(*) Refer Appendix 8 of Manufacturer_Handbook.  

c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations.  
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.  
The Supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such issues within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect.  
The Supplier must propose appropriate risk assessment and resolution plans.  

4.3.2 Europe.  
a. Homologation and safety regulation activities.  
The Supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s) or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.  

b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s).  
The supplier is responsible for getting their part(s), product(s) or system(s) to meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests Supplier to conduct tests as specified in certain UN-ECE at authorized technical service(s). The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  
VinFast also requests Supplier must provide supporting documentation, technical information, including drawings to be submitted to the relevant authorities to get a certification for the EC vehicle type-approval (if any).  
The Supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures that European authorities use to evaluate the compliance of their products with each corresponding UN-ECE regulation.  

c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations.  
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.  
The Supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such issues within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect.  
The Supplier must propose appropriate risk assessment and resolution plans.

RASI is defined as follows:  
**R:** Responsible  
Responsible for proper execution of the activities to fulfill the requirements. She/he is entitled to request the necessary support from other project team members.  

**A:** Approval  
Obligation to decide/responsible for release. If no decider is defined, the person who is responsible for execution (R) is the decider.  

**S:** Support  
Obligated to actively support (even if this requires capacity, expenses, and/or transfer of know-how). This project team member must proactively assist the person responsible for execution (R) in achieving the targets.  

**I:** Information  
Right to information. This team member is entitled to demand information from (R).
