### Emissions & Toxicology  

#### Odor  
- The odor behavior of interior components must be tested as per VDA 270 standards.  
- Tests must be coordinated with the specialist department at VinFast.  
- A target value of <3 for odor according to this specification must be demonstrated using measurements provided by the supplier.  
- Optional test procedures to measure odor behavior can be established according to ISO 12219, Part 7, with expert alignment at VinFast.  

#### Interior Parts Emissions (Bag Method)  
- Emissions from interior components, semi-finished products, auxiliary means, and surface materials (e.g., foils, artificial leather, films) must comply with ISO 12219, Part 2 ('bag method').  
- Measurements must verify fulfillment of target values defined by VinFast before purchase release.

Harmful substances in components and materials.

The emission behavior of the product must be checked on representative components as soon as possible. Emissions of volatile organic compounds in the passenger compartment must be minimized accordingly. Measurements for confirmation of the emission characteristics must be conducted by the supplier in agreement with the VinFast specialist department. Testing must be completed at approved testing institutes listed by VinFast. Additional requirements may be found in project-specific documents such as the Emission Guideline.

Volatile organic compounds of components must be tested according to ISO 12219. Compliance with prohibited substances must be ensured throughout the product lifecycle. Test results must be evaluated by the VinFast specialist department, with written proof of harmlessness regarding volatile compounds' impact on human health available on request. Suppliers must provide VOC and Fogging measurement results for materials before milestone purchase release.

Limits for harmful substances emitted by concerned parts and components in the complete interior compartment are defined in the VIAQ Guideline VFDSXVCVC3401.

**After-sales spare parts should be ready at startup.**  
The spare parts provided by the supplier should be ready at the start of the whole vehicle production.

**Spare Parts Support Cycle.**  
The support period of after-sales spare parts shall not be shorter than 10 years after VINFAST starts production of the whole vehicle, except as specified in this SOR.

**Maintenance Information of After-Sale Spare Parts.**  
The supplier of the first-level assembly should provide VINFAST after-sales engineering department information about the design, use, and maintenance of the assembly before OTS. This includes:  
- BOM table  
- Parts structure and function description  
- Fault diagnosis  
- Special tools for maintenance  
- Relevant technical parameters (installation clearance, parts performance parameters, etc.)
