Scope and purpose.  

VinFast (VinFast Trading and Production Limited Liability Company) have started to design and develop a new vehicle in both BEV and ICE (PREM). 4.2 General CAE Requirements.
Please refer to Appendix 2 titled "CAE-FEM-GENERAL." This document outlines VinFast's General FE Modelling and Analysis Requirements for supporting the Statement of Requirement (SOR). The finite element modeling requirements are utilized to support the development of VinFast projects. 

4.3 Material requirement.
Reference document VFDST00068701 provides specifications for materials suitable for components made of hot-formed steels with AL-SI coating.

4.4 Traceability.
Traceability requirements for components must adhere to VinFast standards VFDST00010502 and VFDST00010501. These standards ensure compliance with robust part specifications and adherence to VinFast's dedicated processes for part tracking.

4.5 Labeling of Parts.  
Suppliers must follow VinFast standard VFDST00000010. Labels must be positioned on parts according to specified diagrams or pictures showing the label setup positions.

5 Testing and Validation and DVP&R.  
DVP and PC test responsibilities follow the RASIC matrix. Both virtual and physical testing for systems, subsystems, and components must align with the Complete Vehicle Specification Book during the concept phase. A detailed hardware test plan must be provided in the DVP&R file and coordinated with the Testing & Validation department. Testing details must include:  
- Declaration of standards, norms, and legal requirements.  
- Detailed descriptions of testing setups, including pictures.  
- Identification of internal/external testing locations.  
- Timeline of testing activities in the form of a project plan.  
- Specifications of tested part variants.  
- Number of tested components/parts.  

Testing must ensure a Mileage Target Life of 160,000 km or 10 years, whichever comes first.  
For further details, refer to the documents:  
- VFDSXVCVC2401-CSUV_ICE_Appendix_4_DVP_Design Verification Plan.  
- VFDSXVCVC1401-CSUV_BEV_Appendix_4_DVP_Design Verification Plan.

6.3.1 North America.

The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., European Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS). The supplier must provide and/or support documentation to be submitted to relevant authorities.

The supplier is responsible for "self-certifying" their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before those products can be offered for sale. VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain FMVSS/CMVSS. The test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance. 

The supplier should be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) used by USA/Canada Governments to evaluate the compliance of their products with each FMVSS/CMVSS. 
(*) Refer to Appendix 8 of Manufacturer_Handbook.

Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may determine that non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced. The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast within five working days after determining the existence of non-compliance or a safety-related defect. The supplier must propose a risk assessment and resolution plan.

6.3.2 Europe.

The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.

The supplier is responsible for ensuring their part(s), product(s), or system(s) meet applicable UN-ECE regulations. VinFast requires suppliers to conduct tests at authorized technical services. The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificates must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.

VinFast also requires suppliers to provide supporting documentation, technical information, including drawings, to be submitted to relevant authorities for certification for the EC vehicle type-approval (if any).

The supplier should be familiar with the laboratory test procedures used by European authorities to evaluate the compliance of their products with UN-ECE regulations.

Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may determine that non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced. The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast within five working days after determining the existence of non-compliance or a safety-related defect. The supplier must propose a risk assessment and resolution plan.

Obligation to decide / responsible for release. If no decider is defined, the person who is responsible for execution R is the decider.

- S: Support.  
Obligated to actively support (even if this requires capacity, expenses, and/or transfer of know-how). This project team member must proactively assist the person responsible for execution R in achieving the targets.

- I: Information.  
Right to information. This team member is entitled to demand information from R.
