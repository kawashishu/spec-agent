## 2. General
### 2.1 Part Number Structure
VinFast uses 2 main types of Part numbers (see 2.1.1 & 2.1.2) and additional types of Part numbers with suffixes (see 2.1.3). Depending on specific program/project, Part number types may be used and applied/adapted according to specific decision/approval.

#### 2.1.1 Part number with structure of 11 digits
The structure of part numbers with 11 digits is defined as follows:
1. Identical format for part numbers used in E-Scooter and Car.
2. 03 digits: The group material describes the module the part is located in the vehicle (see 4.2). Examples include:
   - CHS = Chassis.
   - BIW = Body in White.
3. 03 digits: Define for color codes (see 5.2).
4. 08 digits: The base number increases incrementally and forms, with the group material, a unique identifier across the whole PLM system (see 2.2).

2.1.2 Part number with structure of 13 digits.
The structure of part numbers with 13 digits is similar to that of 11 digits, with the addition of 2 digits XY. 

Definition of XY digits:
- **Y: Release status** – From A…Z  
   - **A** – Before or at PR Release  
   - **B, C, D → Z** – After PR Release if changes occur in Fit-Form-Function.  

- **X: Suppliers** – Consider changing PN if the supplier changes, confirmed by Purchasing.  
   - **A** – Representative to the 1st supplier. X runs from A→Z (except in specific/special cases) sequentially, based on ECR.  

Specific/special cases:  
- **P:** Prototype Part/Prototype suppliers. Example: BIW70003011PA.  
- **S:** Soft Tool parts. Example: BIW70003011SA.  
- **H:** In-House Parts (parts both manufactured in-house and purchased from suppliers). Example: BIW70003011HA.  
- **N:** Non-saleable parts (not yet PSW approved, such as not yet graded). Example: BIW700030011NA. This distinguishes it from production parts with suffix AA.

## 2.1.3 Part number structure with suffix digits

In some cases, it is necessary to build the documents in parallel to part development. However, parts may be released before the documents are completed, such as Measurement Points. The difference in completion times between parts and documents causes additional workload for engineering and other parties through the ECR process. To address this, a new related part can be created with suffix digits as illustrated by the example/lesson learnt below:

### Example/lesson learnt 01 – a proposal from EDAG for VF9 project (CE approved):
1. **Create Measurement Point (MP) parts** with suffix "_MP" to store MP information (e.g., BIW80200100_MP/01; BEX8010000_MP/01).
2. **Create a separate Measurement Point (MP) Structure** under the vehicle hierarchy VHC "Vehicle Complete" to store MP files. This allows the GD&T team to manage and control MP files on their own, reducing dependency on engineering support.
3. The **release process can begin with GD&T team involvement** and limit participation to "interested" parties. No revision of the part number is needed.
4. **Measurement Point (MP) Files appear in purchasing** as an "I-part" in the BOM for visibility.

**Example/Lesson Learned 01 – A Proposal from EDAG for VF9 Project (CE Approved):**

3. The release process can be initiated by the GD&T team with only "interested" parties involved. No revision of part number is needed.
4. MP files with the purchasing level "I-part" in BOM can be displayed.
