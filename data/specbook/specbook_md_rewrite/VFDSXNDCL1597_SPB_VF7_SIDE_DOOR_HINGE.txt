### Technical requirements summary (1)

**Refer to DVP for Targets and acceptance criteria.**

#### Material:
- Refer IMDS document for: IMDS material data sheets have to be submitted into VinFast IMDS ID according to the official VinFast IMDS Guideline (Testing & Validation) and VFDST00007601.
- Recycling & Recoverability – UN Regulation No. 133.
- Prohibition & Restriction of substances – Vietnam Law No. 06/2007/QH12.
- Prohibition & Restriction of substances – GADSL.
- Prohibition & Restriction of substances – California Proposition 65.
- Prohibition & Restriction of substances – Annex XIV, XVII of Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006.
- Prohibition & Restriction of substances – Annex II of Directive 2000/53/EC.

#### Functional / Performance:
- Refer to DVP DVPRXNDCL1597, SOR VFSRACDCL1386, VFDST00000013 – General Tolerances and 2D drawings for:
  - Dimensional & Profile Tolerances, overall function, opening angle, weight management etc.
- Durability test to be carried out 100,000 times of reciprocating Door opening and closing test with maximum opening angle.

**Testing Doors:** VFDST00009100.
1. Shaft 50N load: Axis direction: the axial clearance is less than 0.05mm; Axis normal direction: the axial normal direction is less than 0.05mm.
2. Turning moment: Rotation moment 0.2 ~ 1.5 N·m.
3. Shaft holding torque: the shaft holding torque is more than 20 N·m.
4. Strength test: Apply 150 N·m load in direction a, with elastic deflection less than 5° and permanent deflection less than 2°.
5. Power on: The shaft is under 50 N·m load and the resistance is below 10 Ω.

**Rigidity:**
1. Apply force to fully open and closed state, apply 500 N·m load to test deflection curve and deformation part. Elastic deflection: below 2.0mm/1m, permanent deflection: below 0.2mm/1m.
2. Apply force to fully open and closed state, apply 750 N·m load, no damage to each part.

**Compressive strength:**
- Install the test object on the fixture and carry out the compression strength test (as shown in the figure). 
  1. The front and rear load of the car body is more than 13 kN, and the parts are not damaged.

**Actuation angle:** Above 30°.  

**Tensile strength:**

1. Front and rear direction of vehicle body:  
   a) There is no damage to the parts with the front and rear load of 14.3 KN or above.

   d) Actuation angle: More than 30°.
