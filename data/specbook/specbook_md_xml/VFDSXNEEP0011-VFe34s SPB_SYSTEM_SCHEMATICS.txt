**II.1 - Bus architecture for VFe34S Plus.** 

The bus architecture includes a variety of cables: twisted pairs, standard leads, shielded cables (Koax), and separators defined in the wire harness plan. It integrates several types of communication protocols such as Body CAN (B-CAN), Diag CAN, Info CAN, PT CAN, CH CAN, FlexRay, FA CAN, LP CAN, LIN, and LVDS.

**Key Components Include:**
- **Body Modules:** BCM contains LIN1, LIN2, LIN4, WLC, RLS, SCM, BAS, FKS.
- **Vehicle Control Systems:** RCU for Electric Stability Control, SAS for Steering, VCU, and primary OBD connector.
- **Power Units:** PDM powers multimedia display units (MDU), IPC-TBox-MHU, and peripherals including HUD and CPD.
- **Heating Ventilation:** Includes CHM, EAC, DCDC, OBC, and AGS for advanced air and cabin heating systems.
- **Lighting Systems:** Assembly integrates HL, RL, RLB variations and animated lamps controlled via Private CAN.
- **Radar Sensors:** Multiple radar systems for corners, blind spots, and frontal zones combined with ultrasonic sensors.
- **Multimedia and Communication:** MHU multimedia head unit, SVM cameras, FCAM frontal camera, and additional rear-facing modules.
</page>

### II.2 - Bus architecture VFe34S Eco

The section provides an extensive schematic representation of the bus architecture for the VFe34S Eco model. Key components, connections, and systems are visually outlined as follows:

1. **Cable Types:** Connections in the harness include twisted pair, standard lead, and shielded cable (Koax). Specific separators and connectors are to be defined in the wire harness plan.
2. **CAN Systems:** Various bus communication protocols such as Info CAN, PT CAN, CH CAN, FlexRay, FA CAN, LP CAN, LIN, LVDS, and Body CAN (B-CAN) are utilized.
3. **Modules and Sensors:**
   - Body control module (BCM) controlling interior systems like windows and lights.
   - Climate control unit (CCU) manages HVAC functionalities.
   - Airbag control module (ACM) ensures safety systems operation.
   - Steering column module (SCM) provides steering-related functionalities.
   - Battery sensor (BAS) to monitor power levels.
   - Interior temperature sensor (ITSe) for climate adjustments.
   - Rain light sensor (RLS) for automated headlamp adjustments.
   - Anti-pinch module (APM) ensures safe power window usage.
4. **Information Displays:**
   - Multimedia head unit (MHU) integrates IPC and TBox features.
   - Head-up display (HUD) enhances driving visibility.
   - Child presence detector (CPD) ensures safety for rear passengers.
5. **Communication Protocols:** The schematic uses Body-CAN, PT-CAN, CH-CAN, Info-CAN for rapid and coordinated interactions between components.
6. **Advanced Components:**
   - Occupant classification sensors (OCS).
   - Vehicle control unit (VCU) supporting high-voltage CAN systems (HV-CAN 500k).
   - Electric slippery control with sensor clusters and integrated park brakes.
   - Active noise generator (AVAS).
   - Hands-on detection (HOD) for steering.
7. **Drive Units:**
   - Front and rear drive units (EDS-F and EDS-R).
   - On-board charger (OBC) and high-voltage/lower-voltage (HV/LV) transformator.
   - Various electronics for vehicle performance and functionality, including gear shifter (GS) and yaw rate sensors (YSS).
8. **Lighting Systems:** 
   - Headlamp left and right (HL L and HL R).
   - Rear light tailgate assembly (RLT L&R) and rear signature lights.
   - Animation lamps for front light assemblies.
9. **Radar and Camera Systems:** 
   - Front (SRR_FR, SRR_FL) and rear radar (SRR_RR, SRR_RL) support safety systems.
   - Driver monitor camera (DMS) for monitoring driving attention.
   - Blindspot sensors and fish-eye cameras for front, rear, and side views.

This architecture highlights intricate wiring systems and integrated control modules for optimized vehicle operation, passenger safety, and system communication.

**II.3 - Crash signal distribution / CL58**

The diagram illustrates the crash signal distribution and layout for the VFe34S. Key components and functions are connected via twisted pair, standard leads, and shielded cable (Koax), and the detailed harness, connectors, and separators are clearly defined within the wire harness plan. The crash signal loop connects vital systems to ensure functionality in critical scenarios.

**Crash Signal Loop VFe34S**  
The crash signal loop ensures communication between the following components:
- ACM (Airbag Control Module) sends the crash signal.
- BCM (Body Control Module) receives the crash signal.
- MHU (Multimedia Head Unit) integrates crash data for other functions.
- VCU (Vehicle Control Unit) processes crash inputs.
- BMS (Battery Monitoring System) ensures proper power management during crashes.

**CL58 Layout VFe34S**
In the CL58 layout for the VFe34S:
- The system includes components such as WL FR, WL RR, WL RL, and DSB, integrated into the wiring.
- A background light for the CL58 system links elements like:
  - GS, the gear shifter.
  - EPB switch.
  - ETG interior switch, exclusive for VFe34S Plus versions.
  - Switch block center console integrates these inputs for coordinated operation.

The CL58 layout also highlights the focus on background light and linkages in the VFe34S electrically tailored designs.

**Additional Notes:**
- ETG systems are reserved exclusively for VFe34S Plus versions.
- Connections between individual components ensure effective communication and operational reliability within the crash signal distribution.

The OBD (On-Board Diagnostics) system for the VFe34S is presented with detailed wiring and connections. Two types of OBD plugs are shown: 

1. **OBD Plug Serial Production:** 
   - This configuration is designed for the serial production version.
   - Displays pin connections supporting diagnostic protocols such as D-CAN, CH-CAN, Body-CAN, Info-CAN, and PT-CAN.
   - Uses Cl.30 and Cl.31 for the power supply.

2. **OBD Plug 2 Development and Testing:** 
   - This setup is intended for development and testing purposes.
   - Enhanced connections include BCM LIN 1, BCM LIN 2, and VCU LIN 1 alongside the standard CAN protocols.
   - BCM LIN 2 is specifically utilized in the VFe34S Plus version.

### Key Elements:
- **CAN Protocols:** D-CAN, CH-CAN, Body-CAN, Info-CAN, and PT-CAN are integrated into both OBD configurations, supporting vehicle diagnostics.
- **LIN Connections:** BCM LIN 1, BCM LIN 2, and VCU LIN 1 provide communication for advanced electronic components.
- **XGW Integration:** The XGW (Gateway) module serves as a communication hub between the different networks.

The diagram provides a structured representation of pin alignments for both serial and testing connectors, ensuring compatibility for production and development phases.

III.1 - Prefusebox 12V power distribution VFe34S.

The prefuse box is directly positioned beside the battery's positive terminal. This system distributes power to critical components, including:

1. DC/DC converter labeled FP04/60A MIDI connected to EPS1.
2. LV PTC heater associated with FP07/125A MIDI.
3. Front fuse box (UEC) with FP03/200A MEGA.
4. Interior fuse box IEC (base) linked to FP02/200A MEGA.
5. Interior fuse box IEC-R with FP06/60A MIDI.
6. IDB base functions involving FP05/100A MIDI.
7. Connection from FP08/05A MIN to BAS.

Additional details:
- HV/12V DCDC integrated within PDU 3-in-1.
- Configurations include both 12V GND and PE grounding terminals via Cl.31 (chassis).
- EPS system operable from Cl.31 M connection.
- IDB is accessible through a two-pole connector labeled pin 1.

This layout serves as a structured power distribution system, ensuring functionality across multiple fuse points.

III.2 - UEC Fusebox engine room VFe34S.

The UEC Fusebox handles electrical power distribution within the engine room and is linked to the pre-fuse box located near the battery. It supports various functions including cooling systems, lighting, and ADAS backups.

Summary of Connections and Components:

- **Battery and Pre-fuse-box:** Located directly beside the battery.
- **Major Functions Linked:**
  - **Engine room components:** Includes HVAC systems, valve systems, pumps, and wipers.
  - **Lighting control:** Handling beam lamps, fog lamps, and turn lights.
  - **Cabin box BEV:** Supports cabin power distribution networks.
  - **ADAS Backup:** Enables redundancy for level-specific ADAS functions (L1, L2, M1, M2).
  - **Cooling fan relay:** Provides efficient thermal management for the system.

Key Fuse Connections and Types:

- **Fuse Types:** Includes MINI, MICRO, JCASE, MIDI, and MEGA fuses.
- **Highlighted fuses:** 
  - **FU_14/30A MINI:** Supports control relays among other circuits.
  - **FP03/MIDI 200A:** Used for high-power distribution needs.

Special Relay and Distribution Systems:

- **VCU Main Relay Control:** Manages voltage control and ensures relay activations.
- **EDS Systems:** Includes water pump relays for both primary and battery cooling purposes.

Additional Notes:

Fuse value checks are critical for verifying correctness, especially for new specifications concerning the water pumps for VFe34S Plus models.

### III.3 - IEC_F Interior Fuse Box VFe34S.

The diagram illustrates the fuse connections in the IEC_F interior fuse box for the VFe34S model. The connections include several key components, such as the battery and pre-fuse-box, and the following fuse assignments:

#### Fuse Assignments:
1. **FP02/200A MIDI:** Connects to the battery.
2. **FI_07/30A JCASE:** Provides power to Cl.30_APM_1.
3. **FI_06/30A JCASE:** Provides power to Cl.30_APM_2.
4. **FI_05/30A JCASE:** Is connected to Cl.30_ETG power.
5. **FI_25/20A MINI:** Is assigned to Cl.30_SHVU-F Pin 1.
6. **FI_03/60A JCASE:** Connected to Cl.30_SHVU-F Pin 15.
7. **FI_24/30A MINI:** Supplies power to Cl.30_MHU_Lower 1P and 2P.
8. **FI_23/30A MINI:** Supplies power to Cl.30_MHU_Lower 1Q and 2Q.
9. **FI_27/7.5A MINI:** Assigned to Cl.30_XGW.
10. **FI_30/10A MINI:** Supplies Cl.30_SCM and multifunction switches (MFS).
11. **FI_28/7.5A MINI:** Provides power to Cl.30_APM_sig and Cl.30_FKS.
12. **FI_29/7.5A MINI:** Is allocated to Cl.30_HUD and Cl.30_OBDII.
13. **FI_11/60A JCASE:** Supplies Cl.30_XGW and Cl.30_GS.

#### Specialized Circuits:
1. **Alcohol Lock System:** Includes cut line loops for input and output.
2. **E-STOP Switch:** Connected to FI_15 spare (available for use if the new low-level MHU is required).
3. **Trailer Socket:** Cl.30 and Cl.15 connections are established for the ignition power supply.

#### BCM Relay Connections:
1. **Cl.R Relay:** Connected to RI_03/30A/MICRO.
2. **Relay Cl.30s:** Supplies Cl.30s light circuits, including headlight acceleration control, ADAS cameras, and HVAC sensors.

#### VFe34S Plus Features:
- **Cl.15_ADAS Sensors:** Includes connection to MRR and 12 other sensors.
- **Cl.15_SHVU-F / SHVU-R:** Pin connections established for additional features.
- **Automatic Antiglare IRVM:** Included in Cl.15_BCM assignments.

#### Component Connections:
- **LV Service Disconnect Switch:** Connected through HV battery BMS to both inputs and outputs.
- **HV Cabin Heater and HV Compressor:** Connected to Cl.30 ADAS and Cl.15 circuits.

#### General Highlights:
- Alcohol lock and trailer socket systems are integrated with detailed fuse connections for power management.
- Multiple circuits are outlined for supporting advanced electronic components like ADAS, HUD, and MHU in the VFe34S model.

Technical details illustrated reveal significant complexity and integrate safety-critical features and enhanced technology functions to power the interior systems efficiently.
