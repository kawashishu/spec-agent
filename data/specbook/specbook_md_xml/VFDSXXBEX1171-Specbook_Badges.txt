3.7 Testing and validation and DVP&R.

The following aspects are required for testing and validation procedures:
- Testing with declaration of standard, norm, and legal requirement.
- Description of devices used for testing (including setup pictures).
- Identification of the responsible test department.
- Location of testing (internal/external).
- Duration and time flow of testing in the form of a project plan.
- Variant description of the tested part.
- Number of tested components/parts.

Mileage Target Life requirements ensure either 160,000 km or 10 years (whichever comes first).

Test plan regulations and procedures:
The test plan outlines the roles assigned to testing, deadlines, required devices, and the visual state of results alongside testing progress. Test results should always be reliably controlled. The B2P SUP will serve as the contractor for the tests. 

The testing procedures and documentation must receive approval from the corresponding validation engineer and VF component lead engineer. Weekly updates or revisions due to development changes are essential. The B2P SUP must ensure VinFast can access relevant test protocols/reports and attend tests at any B2P SUP site or their testing centers. Agreement between VinFast and B2P SUP is required for any deviations from approved test procedures.

Any incorrect, incomplete, or inconsistent testing will need to be conducted again at the expense of B2P SUP. Negative test results require re-planned testing, also financed by B2P SUP if determined responsible for the failure.

Data included in the DVP&R:
- Standard, norm, and legal requirement declarations for testing.
- Testing devices descriptions, including setup images.
- Identification of responsible test department.
- Testing location specifications.
- Duration and project plans for testing.
- Variant descriptions of tested parts.
- Quantity of components/parts tested.

Responsibilities of the B2P SUP:
The B2P SUP must use the draft DVP&R provided by the VF testing department. A DVP must be created for every development stage, including prototypes and serial stages if outlined in the master timing chart. Test sequencing and specimen quantities are determined collaboratively with validation engineers and component lead engineers from VF. Upon request, test plans must cater to individual components.
</page>

### 3.7.3.2 Release by test departments
The responsible VF test department will release the components only if the agreed test targets are achieved by the B2P SUP and during the complete vehicle integration tests. 

The B2P SUP must submit positive test reports for all required tests according to DVP&R. In case of any issues (negative test results, wrong parameters/processes, etc.), the B2P SUP must submit a test report, be present upon request, and deliver proposals for solutions to maintain all milestones and targets. 

An OK or NOK result needs to be stated on the first sheet of the report.

### 3.7.3.3 Analyses, test reports
The B2P SUP has to provide measurement reports (VFDST00008900) in electronic format with agreed alignments and measurement concepts for all parts during the prototype generations.

After the individual tests, the test samples have to be analyzed in detail. The measurement and test results are to be proven by means of individual values and documented in a test report. The report has to be submitted to the responsible VF department for release.

Upon request, the results, along with the documentation of the tests carried out (initial sample inspections and inspections parallel to development), are to be submitted to the department responsible for approval. 

Tests carried out parallel to series production must be planned and documented at regular intervals or as required.

### 3.7.4 Test report/information
The following information must be included:
- Description/picture of the part with documentation (part number, change index, materials, production process, additives such as grease, and every deviation from approved serial process).
- All parts must be labeled with a description.
- The reason for testing (e.g., negative test results) along with attachment of earlier test reports.
- Testing with exact naming of testing standard, norm, legal requirement, or exact description of agreed testing procedure.

### 3.7.5 Documentation of the result
The report must include:
- Short description of the result (pictures, tables, detailed results, photo documentation, comparison with earlier test reports, evaluation of result OK/NOK, pictures of the parts in testing position, etc.).
- Comparison with results from the specification book, testing specifications, and legal requirements.
- Recommendations.
- Description of corrective measures or further approaches in case of negative test results.

### 3.7.3.2 Release by Test Departments
The responsible VF test department will approve components only after the agreed test targets are achieved, as verified by the B2P SUP during vehicle integration tests. Positive test reports are required for all tests specified in the DVP&R. For issues such as negative outcomes or incorrect parameters, the B2P SUP must provide detailed reports, solutions, and revised timelines. An OK or NOK result must be listed clearly on the first page of each report.

### 3.7.3.3 Analyses and Test Reports
The B2P SUP is responsible for submitting measurement reports (VFDST00008900) electronically, according to agreed alignments and concepts for all parts during prototype generations. Individual tests must be analyzed comprehensively, with documented measurement results included in the test report. Reports, along with parallel development inspections, should be delivered to the VF release department upon request. Series production tests must be planned and documented at regular intervals or when necessary.

### 3.7.4 Test Report Information
Test reports must contain:
- Description or picture of the part with documentation, including part number, change index, materials, production process, and any deviations from the approved serial process.
- Labeling of all parts with an appropriate description.
- Explanation for testing reasons, e.g., negative results, with earlier test reports attached.
- Exact naming of testing standards, norms, legal requirements, or detailed descriptions of agreed testing procedures.

### 3.7.5 Documentation of Results
Documentation of test results should include:
- Brief result description with supporting data such as pictures, tables, comparisons with earlier reports, and a detailed evaluation (OK/NOK).
- Comparison of results against the specification book, testing specifications, and applicable legal requirements.
- Recommendations and corrective measures for addressing negative test results.

### 3.7.6 Archival Storage of Tested Parts
Testing parts may only be scrapped with written agreement from the VinFast test department. On request, the complete testing documentation (including reports, parts, and descriptions) must be made available to the test engineer.

### 3.7.7 Virtual Testing
B2P SUP should utilize suitable calculation methods and create adequately detailed models for optimizing component properties as defined in technical specifications. FEM calculations are necessary to validate mechanical properties such as rigidity, strength, and dimensional stability. Injection molding should be optimized via simulations, and FEM models made readily available upon request without extra costs. Documentation of tests must be calculative (e.g., FEM analysis) before final tool dimensioning.

### 3.7.8 Component Testing
Component testing requirements are outlined in the DVP and must be agreed upon with VF, VinFast, and B2P SUP. Adherence to the specifications in the DVP is mandatory.

### 3.7.9 Mechanical Integration Tests
Mechanical integration testing will encompass:
- Climatic Change Test (VFDST00046100)
- Sunlight Simulation (VFDST00053800)
- Mounting/Dismounting Test (VFDST00009000)
- Vibrational and Temperature Tests (VFDST00062600)

There must be no distortion or functional disturbance throughout the entire functional temperature range as per the VTS specifications. Material behavior due to differences in thermal expansion coefficients must remain reliable post tests. Tolerances for flush and gap measurements post climatic and sunlight testing must not be exceeded.

### 4 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements

#### 4.3.1 North America
a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., European, Federal motor vehicle safety standards (FMVSS) and Canadian motor vehicle safety standards (CMVSS).
The supplier must provide and/or support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.

b. Self-certification and test report(s):
The supplier is responsible for “self-certifying” their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before those products can be offered for sale.
VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain of the FMVSS/CMVSS. The test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.
The supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) that USA/Canada Governments use to evaluate the compliance of their products with each FMVSS/CMVSS.
(*) Refer Appendix 8 of Manufacturer_Handbook.

c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations:
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.
The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast on such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect.
</page>

4.3.2 Europe.

a. Homologation and safety regulation activities.
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.

b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s).
The supplier is responsible for getting their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before those products can be offered for sale.
VinFast requests the supplier to conduct tests as specified in certain UN-ECE at authorized technical services. The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.
VinFast also requests the supplier to provide supporting documentation, technical information, including drawings, to be submitted to the relevant authorities to get certification for the EC vehicle type-approval (if any).

The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast on such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of non-compliance or a safety-related defect.
The supplier must propose appropriate risk assessment and resolution plans.

5 Deliverables

5.1 Technical deliverables.
Work Interface Chart (RASI). The main supplier obligations are described in the work interface chart (RASI). The status of the supplier ("Built to Print") is defined below System / Component Specification.  
RASI is defined as followed:  
**R: Responsible**  
Responsible for proper execution of the activities to fulfill the requirements. She/he is entitled to request the necessary support from other project team members.  
**A: Approval**  
Obligation to decide/responsible for release. If no decider is defined, the person who is responsible for execution (R) is the decider.  
**S: Support**  
Obligated to active support (even if this requires capacity, expenses, and/or transfer of know-how). This project team member must proactively assist the person responsible for execution (R) in achieving the targets.  
**I: Information**
