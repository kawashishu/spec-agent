## 3.4.1 Additional Material Requirements

### Heat Resistance:
- Components must withstand long-term temperatures of 150°C (minimum) and short-term temperatures of 180°C (minimum). 
- For temperatures above this range, additional insulators or reflectors may be implemented, subject to confirmation during complete vehicle validation.

### Burning Behaviour:
- Components must utilize flame retardant fabric for sound insulation located in the engine compartment. 
- Flame testing must follow VFDST00032600 (adhering to UN R 118 and NFPA 556), with tests conducted per DIN 75200 standards. 
- Maximum burning rate allowed is 100 mm per minute. Burn distance during 15 seconds of flame application must not exceed 38 mm.

### Water and Splash Resistance:
- Minimal water pass-through is permitted.

## Performance Specification: 3.4.2 NVH and Driving Comfort

- The exterior trim system for VINFAST vehicles must align with reference vehicle standards to ensure driving comfort and prevent disruption from operational sounds (e.g., wind noise, squeak, and rattle). 
- Measures must be implemented to prevent noise generation and absorb unwanted sounds during operation. 
- Components must meet gas and water-tightness requirements to ensure acoustical tightness. 
- Design must eliminate chattering or squeaking effects, particularly at high vehicle speeds or humidity levels, through optimal material pairing and surface coating processes.
- Mechanical stability across all speed ranges is mandatory. Flat surfaces must include stiffening structures to maintain stability over the vehicle’s lifetime. 
- Components must resist deformation, shrinkage, and the effects of temperature, moisture, dirt, UV radiation, and adhere to specified quality, including compatibility with appropriate adhesives.
- Fixation points (e.g., screws, clips) must be air-tight unless exceptions are approved by a function specialist.
- Reduction of aerodynamic resistance and optimization of aero-acoustic properties are required.
- Burrs related to manufacturing processes must be avoided unless approved by a function specialist.

The supplier is responsible for ensuring:
- No amplification of wind noise or vibrations on BIW.

3.4.5 Emissions & Toxicology.

**Harmful substances in components and materials.**  
The emission behavior of the product must be examined on representative components as soon as possible. Emissions of volatile organic compounds (VOC) in the passenger compartment must be minimized accordingly. Required measurements for confirmation of emission characteristics must be conducted by the supplier and agreed upon with the specialist department of VinFast. Measurements should be carried out by testing institutes approved by VinFast. Suppliers are recommended to obtain an overview of all approved testing institutes from VinFast. Additional requirements may be found in project-specific documents (e.g., Emission Guideline).  

Volatile organic compounds of components must be checked according to ISO 12219. The conformance to prohibited substances must be ensured throughout the complete product life cycle.  

Test results are evaluated by VinFast's specialist department. In all cases, confirmation of the harmlessness of the volatile compounds for human health must be provided in written form upon demand. Before the milestone purchase release, suppliers must deliver measurement results related to VOC and material fogging performance.

### 3.4.6 Recycling

#### Recycled Materials
When selecting materials, attention must focus on reducing the variety of materials and ensuring compatibility with available recycling procedures ("proven recycling technologies"). Secondary raw materials such as post-consumer recyclates and post-production recyclates (e.g., recycled polymeric PET fiber, recycled textiles, recycled foams, recycled thermoplastics, etc.) must be preferred for availability in the market. Recycled materials must be marked according to parts marking standards in this specification book and reported to the environmental compliance expert team at VinFast.

#### Recycling Scheme
This chapter establishes general project-wide requirements for recycling and repair schemes during component development. Design for recycling of parts and components is required to enable cost-effective end-of-life recycling.

The recycling scheme provided by the contractor must include:
- A simple layout for the possible recycling process of the component.
- Information about preferred recycling partners and cost estimation.

The recycling scheme must be submitted to the environmental expert team at VinFast before the milestone PTO (Production Try-Out).

### 3.4.7 Evaporative Emissions
To meet legal standards for permeability, suppliers must provide evaporative emission calculations, measurements, and validation results from fuel component testing.

#### Testing Procedure
Testing of fuel systems—such as tanks, filler pipes, connectors, carbon canisters, fuel pipes, and fuel feeds to ICE—must align with VinFast experts and the target market. Key requirements include:
- Fulfillment of total evaporative emission target values as defined by VinFast.
- Measurements according to Vietnam National Technical Standard No. QCVN 86: 2015/BGTVT or UN Regulation No. 83, Type 4 test (Annex 7).

#### Hot-Soak Target
- Target value for hot-soak evaporative emissions of ICE: 0.15 g HC/test.
- Testing fuel: E5 reference fuel (5% ethanol / 95% gasoline).

---

### 3.4.8 Drainage and Depollution
Complete fluid drainage is required for safe and environmentally friendly end-of-life vehicle treatment. Fluid niches and pockets must be avoided to ensure fluid removal.

The drainage scheme must include:
- A virtual or physical test protocol.
- Information on types of drained fluids.
- Amount of drained fluids (weight-% and absolute mass in kg).

This information must be submitted to VinFast’s environmental expert team before the PTO milestone (Production Try-Out).

3.7.1 Test Plan  
The test plan regulates by whom and when the testing will be conducted, specifying necessary testing devices. It tracks testing results visually and monitors progress. Test results must remain controlled at all times.  

- The B2P SUP is contracted to conduct the tests.  
- Test procedures and documentation require approval from corresponding validation engineers and VF component lead engineers.  
- Weekly updates or revisions are mandatory if new data or changes are required.  
- The B2P SUP must provide all relevant test reports/protocols to VinFast for verification.  

Deviations from approved testing procedures require written agreement between VinFast and the B2P SUP. Costs for retesting due to issues caused by B2P SUP (e.g., incorrect parts, missing components) must be borne by the supplier.

- Naming of the responsible test department.  
- Identification of test location (internal/external).  
- Testing timeline and duration in project plan format.  
- Descriptions of tested parts and components.  
- Details on the number of tested parts.

The supplier must weekly update the test plan, capturing points such as validation progress.

3.7.3 Testing  
**General**  
The B2P SUP must provide a timely and detailed testing plan consistent with the vehicle timing chart and ensure worst-case component/system variants are tested. Mechanical and electrical tests must be combined wherever feasible to avoid redundancy.

The supplier must submit positive reports for all required tests per DVP&R. If VinFast orders parts for testing on their own, the detailed description of the parts must be attached.  

3.7.6 Archival storage of tested parts  
Testing parts are only allowed to be scrapped if the VinFast test department agrees to that. On the demand of the test engineer, the complete testing documentation (reports, parts, and descriptions) must be presented.  

3.7.7 Virtual testing  
Suitable calculation methods are to be used and adequately detailed calculation models created by the B2P SUP for the purpose of securing, verifying, and effectively optimizing the component properties defined in the technical specification, test procedures, and valid laws/regulations. The requirements related to the mechanical properties of the component, such as rigidity, strength, dimensional stability under heat, and passive safety, are to be secured by means of FEM calculations.  

The tool-dimensioning for the injection molding is to optimize by corresponding simulations. On request, the operative calculation models that represent the respective development status are to be provided.

3.7.8 Component testing.  
The detailed required testing will be defined in the DVP. The DVP has to be agreed between VF, VinFast, and the B2P SUP, and will be binding.  

3.7.9 Mechanical integration tests.  
The following tests will be done as mechanical integration tests in a complete vehicle:  
- Climatic change test (VFDST00046100).  
- Sunlight simulation (VFDST00053800).  
- Mounting/dismounting (VFDST00009000).  
- Vibration combined with temperature (VFDST00062600).  

Basically, no distortion and no functional disturbance on the whole functional temperature range according to VTS is allowed. In particular, the behavior of materials with high and very different thermal expansion coefficients has to be kept stable.  

The tolerances of the flush and gap plan after the climate test and sun simulation must not be exceeded.  

If VinFast orders parts for testing on their own, the detailed description of the parts has to be attached.  

Parts needed for testing activities at VinFast test department must be provided by the B2P SUP free of charge.
