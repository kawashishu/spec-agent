- Components must maintain full functionality for at least 10 years or 160,000 km, whichever comes first.  
- Designed for high-volume production.  
- Maintenance-free over vehicle lifespan.  
- Emit no disturbing noises.  
- Fully functional over 30,000 open-close hood cycles.  
- Use standard and common parts where reasonable and possible.  

**Additional Requirements:**  
Refer to: General Engineering Requirements VinFast.

- Certification requirements simulate 10 years of exposure or 160,000 km under customer conditions.  
- Suppliers must conduct simulated durability tests and issue corresponding test reports.

- Suppliers must conduct subsystem tests and certify parts/subsystems as required by VinFast standards.  
- Sub-supplier or third-party testing may be utilized if approved by VinFast.

  - System-specific requirements.

### Requirements  

#### 3.2.1.1 Quality Requirements  

- Parts validation tests must be conducted in laboratories meeting national qualification standards.  
- Interior components must comply with VINFAST's VOC limit and odor rating requirements for interior assembly.  
- Metal parts and systems must meet anti-corrosion requirements for complete vehicles and parts.  

#### 3.2.1.2 Technical Requirements  

- Assembly manufacturing must adhere to approved drawings and technical documents, following QC/T 29101 "Automobile Control Cable Assembly" standards.  
- Cable assemblies must operate effectively under temperatures between -40℃ and 75℃.  
- Metal parts must be free of cracks, bruises, corrosion, deformations, or defects affecting performance.  
- Non-metal parts must have uniform, smooth color with no bubbles, cracks, or other manufacturing defects.  
- Core wires must be coated with grease compliant with GB/T 491 "Calcium Base Grease.”  
- Cable assembly load efficiency must be at least 65%, and sample load efficiency must be at least 70%.  
- Coating and chemical treatment layers must comply with QC/T 625 standards for automobiles.  
- Cable assemblies must show no cracks, melting, or defects after bending at a minimum working radius of 360° at both +75℃ and -40℃.  

#### 3.2.1.3 Overall Function  

Development targets include:  
- Prevention of functional and qualitative issues from known systems.  
- Use of standard and common parts wherever practical.  
- Process reliability during production, transport, and assembly.  
- Minimized effort in production sequence: production, packaging, and assembly.  
- Prevention of displacement during production and handling.  
- Integration of all relevant partners into the process from the concept phase (Simultaneous Engineering).  
- Reduction in costs compared to predecessor models.  
- Ensured functionality within the temperature range of -30℃ to +85℃ (-5℃ for the Vietnam market).  
- Support for the function of IP (Intellectual Property).

**4.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements.**

**4.3.1 North America.**
- **Homologation and safety regulation activities.**
  The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., European, Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS). The supplier must provide and/or support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.

- **Self-certification and test report(s).**
  The supplier is responsible for "self-certifying" their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before offering those products for sale. VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain FMVSS/CMVSS. The test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance. Suppliers should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) that USA/Canada Governments use to evaluate compliance of their products with FMVSS/CMVSS.
  (*) Refer to Appendix 8 of the Manufacturer Handbook.

- **Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations.**
  Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced. The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect. The supplier must propose an appropriate risk assessment and resolution plan.

**4.3.2 Europe.**
- **Homologation and safety regulation activities.**
  The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.

- **Test report(s) and Certificate(s).**
  The supplier is responsible for getting their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before offering those products for sale. VinFast requests the supplier conduct tests as specified in certain UN-ECE at authorized technical service(s). The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.

### 5.1 Technical deliverables  
The main supplier obligations are described in the Work Interface Chart (RASI). The status of the supplier (“System Development Supplier”) is defined in System / Component Specification.

**R:** Responsible
