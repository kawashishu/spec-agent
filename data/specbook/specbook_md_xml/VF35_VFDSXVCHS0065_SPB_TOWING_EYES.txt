FRONT VIEW:

- The front view specifies that the component features Rd24x1/8'' threads with a tolerance of -7e after machining and -8g after surface treatment. The surface treatment follows VFDST00001002NT standards. The part is hardened and tempered to 900-1000 N/mm². 
- Die closure tolerance: +1.2mm to -0.3mm.
- Forging flash removal ensures a tolerance of +0.00 to +1.0 unless otherwise specified.
- Hardness in the core is measured between 285 HV 30 and 315 HV 30, per DIN EN ISO 6570-1:2006-03.

TOP VIEW:

- The top view highlights section views A-A and B-B for detailed cross-sectional measures and specifications. 
- Measures include 0.6 for section A-A and up to 5 max for section B-B.

TOWING EYE REQUIREMENTS:

1. **Interface with BIW**:
   - Threads: Rd24x1/8'' - 7e after machining, Rd24x1/8'' - 8g after surface treatment.
   - Remark: Round-thread requested.

2. **Vehicle weight for testing**:
   - Requirement: 24,400 N.

3. **Hardness test**:
   - Standard: DIN EN ISO 6570-1:2006-03.
   - Hardness range: 285 HV 30 - 315 HV 30.

4. **Frequency of tensile and hardness tests**:
   - One test per production batch and one test per material batch.

5. **Material**:
   - SCM440 used for manufacturing.
</page>

3.7 Testing and Validation and DVP&R  
Testing and validation responsibilities follow RASIC guidelines. DVP (virtual and physical testing) for system, sub-system, and component levels must align with the Complete Vehicle Specification Book during the concept phase. A detailed test plan for hardware testing is documented in the DVP&R file and coordinated with the Testing & Validation department:  
- Testing must adhere to declared standards, norms, and legal requirements.  
- A description of testing setups, including pictures of the devices used, must be provided.   
- Details on the location where testing occurred (internal/external) should be included.   
- Testing duration and time flow should be presented as a project plan.  
- Identification of the part variant and description of the part delivered for testing is required.  
- The quantity of tested components/parts must be documented.  

Ensure the Mileage Target Life requirement: 165,000 km or 10 years (whichever comes first).  
Please refer to VFDSXVCHS0065 for further details.  

3.8 Appearance Approval - Applicable for all Visible Parts  
AAR is a PPAP deliverable and described in standard VFDST00038401. This includes specifying the quantity of parts and timing requirements.  
Suppliers are responsible for submitting parts to VF Quality Design on an agreed schedule to obtain OK Shape, OKTG, and AAR approval.  
The process begins during the early phase at the PT Build stage and ensures AAR Approval at LS. Suppliers are required to integrate this activity into their development processes and ED&D costs.

4.3 Homologation and safety regulation requirements.
4.3.1 North America.
a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:
- The supplier must ensure their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with relevant regulations such as FMVSS and CMVSS. This includes providing documentation to relevant authorities.

b. Self-certification and test report(s):
- Suppliers are responsible for “self-certifying” compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS for their products before sale. Test reports to evidence compliance must be submitted to VinFast.
- Suppliers should be familiar with laboratory test procedures used by USA/Canada governments to evaluate product compliance. Refer to Appendix 8 of Manufacturer_Handbook.

c. Duty to notify VinFast of non-compliance:
- A supplier must notify VinFast within five days of discovering non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS or identifying safety-related defects. Risk assessments and resolution plans must be provided.

4.3.2 Europe.
a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:
- Suppliers must ensure their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with UN-ECE regulations.

b. Test report(s) and certificate(s):
- Suppliers must conduct tests as per UN-ECE regulations at authorized technical services. Test results and certificates must be submitted to VinFast.
- Documentation, technical information, and supporting files must be presented to authorities for EC vehicle type-approval, if applicable.

c. Duty to notify VinFast of non-compliance:
- Suppliers must notify VinFast within five days of identifying non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations or discovering safety-related defects. Risk assessments and resolution plans must be proposed promptly.

5.1 Technical deliverables.
Work Interface Chart (RASI). The main supplier obligations are described in the work interface chart (RASI). The status of the supplier (“Built to Print”) is defined below System / Component Specification.

RASI is defined as followed:
- R: Responsible.
  Responsible for proper execution of the activities to fulfill the requirements. She/he is entitled to request the necessary support from other project team members.
- A: Approval.
  Obligation to decide/responsible for release. If no decider is defined, the person who is responsible for execution R is the decider.
- S: Support.
  Obligated to actively support (even if this requires capacity, expenses and/or transfer of know-how). This project team member must proactively assist the person responsible for execution R in achieving the targets.
- I: Information.
  Right to information. This team member is entitled to demand information from R.

### Customer service requirements  
#### After-sales spare parts should be ready at startup  
The spare parts provided by the supplier should be ready at the start of the entire vehicle production.  

#### Spare Parts Support Cycle  
The support period of after-sales spare parts must ensure the lifecycle of the project and meet requirements in GTC after VINFAST ends production of the whole vehicle, except as specified in this SOR.

The supplier of the first-level assembly must provide VINFAST’s after-sales engineering department with the following information before OTS:

- Parts structure and function description.

- Relevant technical parameters (installation clearance, parts performance parameters, etc.).
