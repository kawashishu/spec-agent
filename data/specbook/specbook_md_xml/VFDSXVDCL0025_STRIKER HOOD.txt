5 Description and scope of work.

5.1 Technical requirement.

5.1.1 Project Requirements.

5.1.1.1 Quality Requirements.
- The parts shall be in accordance not only with the drawings and specifications listed here but also with any additional requirements on the SOR. Any items in the drawings, specifications and SOR may be changed in the future, and the parts shall be in accordance with the changed requirements.
- VINFAST determines material selection based on product design, durability, and performance requirements and provides material details for each component.
- The identification on the parts must meet the relevant requirements of bams-040 "Regulations on the identification of auto parts." If there are special requirements, the parts must meet the relevant national requirements.
- Detailed technical requirements of components must meet basic requirements: the performance of body sheet metal parts must conform to the requirements of this standard and the DVP. Parts must strictly adhere to the approved drawings and relevant technical documents.
- Appearance requirements dictate: no shrinkage marks, scratches, or other defects affecting the surface quality of parts. Parts must not have flash burrs on the parting line. Surface color must be uniform, smooth, and without cracks, warping, smudges, or color spots.
- High performance evaluation involves placing the parts in a 90 (±2)°C temperature box for 4 hours.
- Resistance to heat and cold/degeneration testing involves cycling parts from room temperature (23 ±2°C) to low (-30 ±2°C) and high temperatures (70 ±2°C) in a controlled environment, followed by four cycles totaling 24 hours.
- Weather resistance is evaluated using a paint-aging chamber under specified conditions for 450 hours.
- Low-temperature impact testing conforms to ISO 6603-2 standards.

5.1.1.2 Certification.
- Certification requirements simulate 10 years of vehicle exposure or 160,000 km of customer service conditions to ensure product reliability. Suppliers must conduct experiments on components/subsystems and issue reports before higher-level vehicle testing and demonstration.
- VINFAST manages vehicle-level system integration and certification. Suppliers of components/subsystems must adhere to the vehicle certification program.
</page>

- Components must undergo a corrosion durability run to verify corrosion protection for the complete vehicle.  
- Components with metallic coating in visible areas must pass without red rust according to “VFDST00041200 Saltspray.” There should be no failure due to corrosion for at least 10 years.

7.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements.

7.3.1 North America.  
**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:**  
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s) or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., European Federal motor vehicle safety standards (FMVSS) and Canadian motor vehicle safety standards (CMVSS).  
The supplier must provide and/or support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.  

**b. Self-certification and test report(s):**  
The supplier is responsible for “self-certifying” their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests suppliers conduct tests as specified in certain FMVSS/CMVSS. The test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  

- Suppliers should familiarize themselves with the laboratory test procedures (*) used by the USA/Canada Governments to evaluate compliance with each FMVSS/CMVSS.  
(*) Refer to Appendix 8 of Manufacturer_Handbook.  

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations:**  
Notwithstanding certification of a product, a supplier may determine non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it produced.  
The supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of non-compliance or a safety-related defect.  

7.3.2 Europe.  
**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:**  
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.

**b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s):**  
The supplier must get their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests suppliers conduct tests as specified in certain UN-ECE at authorized technical services. The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with an UN-ECE regulation:**  
Notwithstanding product certification, suppliers may determine that non-compliance or a safety-related defect exists per UN-ECE regulations.  
Suppliers must notify VinFast within five working days of determining non-compliance or safety-related defect existence.
