## II. Overview electrical system.

### II.1 - Bus architecture ICE Full.
The ICE Full bus architecture integrates twisted pair wires, standard leads, and shielded cables (Koax) as part of the harness, connectors, and separators. These elements are defined in the wire harness plan. The architecture supports multiple communication protocols, including Info CAN, PT CAN, CH CAN, FlexRay, FA CAN, LP CAN, LIN, LVDS, Body CAN (B-CAN), and Diag CAN. 

Key modules and sensors are integrated:
- **Body control module (BCM)**: Communicates via Body-CAN and BCM-LIN1/2/3.
- **Climate control unit (CCU)**: Manages temperature and connects via CCU-LIN1.
- **Airbag control module (ACM)**: Ensures passenger safety via ACM-LIN connections.
- **Electric Tail Gate (ETG)**: Operates through BCM-LIN connections.
- **Trailer module (TRM)**: Supports vehicle towing capabilities.
- **Seat heating ventilation (SHVU-F and SHVU-R)**: Controls front and rear seat temperatures.
- **Power seat memory (PSM-D)**: Stores seat memory settings.
- **Anti-pinch module (APM)**: Protects window operations.
- **Rain light sensor (RLS)**: Detects environmental conditions.
- **Wireless charger (WLC)**: Enables wireless device charging.
- **Foot kick sensor (FKS)**: Provides foot gesture-based interactions.
- **Multifunction switches (MFS)** and multimedia systems like **MHU**, **IPC TBox**, **MDU**, **HUD**, and cameras (FVC, LSC, RSC, RVC): Deliver interface and imaging solutions with LVDS connections.

Various power distribution points, including ESC, EPS, SAS, EMS, and TCU, manage critical vehicle components such as transmission, stability control, steering, and audio-visual systems. LVDS and CAN are extensively utilized across the architecture.

---

### II.2 - Bus architecture BEV Full.
The BEV Full bus architecture design accommodates twisted pair wires, standard leads, and shielded cables (Koax) defined within the harness plan. Protocols such as Info CAN, PT CAN, CH CAN, FlexRay, FA CAN, LP CAN, LIN, LVDS, Body CAN (B-CAN), and Diag CAN remain integral to communication.

Key features include:
- **Body control module (BCM)**: Linked via Body-CAN and BCM-LIN1/2/3 pathways.
- **Climate control unit (CCU)**: Manages thermal control through CCU-LIN1 communication.
- **Airbag control module (ACM)**: Interfaces with BCM-LIN for occupant protection.
- **Trailer module (TRM)** and **Electric Tail Gate (ETG)**: Functionality supported.
- **Advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS)**: Enhanced safety and control.
- **Occupant classification sensors (OCS_D and OCS_P)**: Detect passenger presence.
- **Active grill shutter (AGS)** and air conditioning systems such as **Cabin heater (CHM)**, **EAC**, and associated valves.
- **Multimedia systems (IPC TBox, MHU, MDU, HUD)**: Connected to LVDS for seamless operation.

Vehicle control and propulsion systems are integrated, including VCU, EDS-F, and EDS-R for drive units, complemented by modules like OBC, DCDC, BMS, and AVAS. Specific attention is given to windshield cameras (WSC), radars (FR, RCR_R, RCR_L), and ultra-sonic sensors (USS1).

Both ICE and BEV architectures prioritize connectivity, modular integration, and advanced features for efficient vehicle management.

II.5 - Cl.15_WUP (wake-up line) connection.

Cl.15_WUP is a switched hardware clamp generated by BCM. Its primary function is to wake up CAN ECU’s during wake-up events. This is used for ECUs with CAN transceivers that cannot be directly awakened via the CAN bus.

Wake-up system ICE:
- Cl.15_WUP_output is generated by the BCM.
- Cl.15_WUP_input is connected to:
  - EMS (Engine Management System).
  - TCU (Transmission Control Unit).

XII. Driver assistance systems.
XII.1 - ADAS C SUV ICE smart.

Info CAN, PT CAN, CH CAN, FlexRay, FA CAN, LP CAN, LIN, LVDS, Body CAN (B-CAN), and Diag CAN are used throughout the system. The following components and connections are included:

- Cl.31 is a ground connection for multiple sensors and components.
- USS1_FSR, USS1_FR, USS4_FL, USS2_FSL, USS4_RL, USS3_RML, USS2_RMR, and USS1_RR are ultrasonic sensors for proximity detection, distributed around the vehicle.
- Outer mirror left includes BSM LED functionality with Cl.31 as ground.
- Outer mirror right includes BSM LED functionality with Cl.31 as ground.
- USS2_RS L and USS1_RS R are additional proximity sensors connected to Cl.31.
- Cl.30s_FI_47 is a fused connection with Cl.31 providing ground.
- Cl.30 FI_20 and Cl.30 FI_19 are power-fed connections integrated in the front radar system.
- Cl.15_1 FI_36, Cl.R FI_51, Cl.30 FI_27, Cl.30s FI_38, and RLS are used for various subsystem power supplies.
- BCM LIN1 connects Body Control Module operations such as lighting.
- BCM operates through connections like Cl.30 FI_27 and Cl.15_1 FI_36.
- ADAS is a central control system for driver assistance components including front radar and windshield camera.
- USS3_FLM and USS2_FRM are ultrasonic sensors connected to B-CAN_H and B-CAN_L for communication.
- Private MRR CAN_H and MRR CAN_L are private CAN networks dedicated to radar communication.
- ESC operates using PT-CAN_H, PT-CAN_L, CH-CAN_H, and CH-CAN_L.
- EPS enhances steering functionality through various CAN channels and LIN networks.
- SAS integrates steering angle sensing using PT-CAN networks and LIN system communication.

Connectivity to advanced sensors is established via GND (ground) and supply (power) networks, including parking-LIN and BSD-LIN connections for blind spot detection.

The MHU subsystem and TRM system are incorporated for enhanced vehicle communication, using GND and power supply connections. FlexRay BP and BN are essential components for high-speed communication in ADAS systems.
