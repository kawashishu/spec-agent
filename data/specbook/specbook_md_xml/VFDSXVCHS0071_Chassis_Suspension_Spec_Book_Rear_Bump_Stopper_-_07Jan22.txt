4 Function Safety, Homologation and Safety Regulations.

Safety is one of the key issues in the development and integration of automotive functionalities. Functional safety is concerned with E/E systems.

4.1 Functional Safety Requirements.
The supplier shall ensure that the developed/manufactured part(s) meet IATF 16949:2016 standard, Quality management system requirements for automotive production. Additionally, the supplier shall ensure that the developed/manufactured part(s) meet ISO 26262:2018 standard, Road vehicles - Functional safety. 

4.2 Deliverables for Functional Safety.
Indicative RASIC for expected deliverables:
- Supplier selection report from requirements 5.4.2.1 and 5.4.2.2 (R: VinFast, I: Supplier).
- Development interface agreement (DIA) resulting from requirement 5.4.3 (R: VinFast, S: Supplier).
- Supplier's project plan from requirement 5.4.3 (A: VinFast, R: Supplier).
- Supplier's safety plan from requirement 5.4.3 (A: VinFast, R: Supplier).
- Functional safety assessment report from 5.4.5.1 to 5.4.5.5 (I: VinFast, R: Supplier).
- Supply agreement from 5.4.6.2 to 5.4.6.3 (R: VinFast, S: Supplier).
- Safety case from requirement 6.4.6 (A: VinFast, R: Supplier).

* FUSA Deliverables Expected Before Nomination:
  - For each safety requirement, the supplier shall provide a supplier acceptance statement or justification if requirements cannot be met.
  - VinFast and supplier shall agree upon a DIA as described in ISO 26262-8 clause 5.

* FUSA Deliverables Expected After Nomination:
  - Supplier shall submit a Safety Plan describing process, activities, resource plans, and documentation as per ISO 26262-2 clause 6.4.3.5.
  - Supplier shall submit a Safety Case detailing outcomes, deviations, and measures consistent with ISO 26262-2 clause 6.5.3.

4.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements.

4.3.1 North America.
a. Homologation and Safety Regulation Activities.
The supplier must ensure their parts, products, or systems comply with FMVSS/CMVSS and provide necessary documentation to relevant authorities.

b. Self-Certification and Test Report(s).
The supplier shall self-certify their parts, products, or systems to comply with FMVSS/CMVSS regulations. Test reports must be submitted to VinFast as evidence of compliance.

c. Duty to Notify VinFast of Non-Compliance.
Suppliers must inform VinFast within five working days of any non-compliance or safety-related defect. The supplier must submit a risk assessment and resolution plan.

4.3.2 Europe.
a. Homologation and Safety Regulation Activities.
Suppliers must ensure compliance with UN-ECE regulations and provide required documentation to authorities.

b. Test Report(s) and Certificate(s).
Suppliers are responsible for meeting applicable UN-ECE regulations before their products can be offered for sale.
