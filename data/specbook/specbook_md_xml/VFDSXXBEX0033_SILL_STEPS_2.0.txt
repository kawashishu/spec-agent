3.7 Testing and validation and DVP&R.  
Testing and validation responsibilities include virtual and physical testing at the system, sub-system, and component levels, aligned with the Complete Vehicle Specification book during the concept phase. The supplier must work with the Testing & Validation department to produce detailed testing plans, reporting details including:

- Declaration of standard, norm, and legal requirement.
- Description of testing setup with pictures and devices used.
- Internal and external test locations.
- Testing duration and timeline (project plan format).
- Part variants and descriptions.
- Number of tested components/parts.
- Submission of PPAP material data sheets (MDS) in IMDS following IMDS Rules and Guidelines IMDS 001 and IMDS Recommendations 002-122. Updates are required for any changes.
- Material data sheets to be submitted to VinFast IMDS ID: 214435.

Mileage Target Life: Ensure durability for 160,000 km or 10 years (whichever comes first). Refer to DVP Sill Steps document VFDSXXBEX0133.

4 Function Safety, Homologation, and Safety Regulations.  
Safety is one of the key issues in the development and integration of automotive functionalities.  
Functional safety is concerned with E/E systems.

The supplier shall ensure that the developed/manufactured parts meet IATF 16949:2016 standards, Quality management system requirements for automotive production.

4.3 Homologation and safety regulation requirements.

4.3.1 North America.
a) Homologation and safety regulation activities:
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations such as European standards, Federal motor vehicle safety standards (FMVSS), and Canadian motor vehicle safety standards (CMVSS). The supplier must provide and/or support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.

b) Self-certification and test report(s):
The supplier is responsible for "self-certifying" their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS regulations before those products can be offered for sale. VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain FMVSS/CMVSS. Test report(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  

The supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) that USA/Canada governments utilize to evaluate the compliance of their products with FMVSS/CMVSS.

(*) Refer to Appendix 8 of the Manufacturer Handbook.
</page>

<page number="11">
c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations.
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with an FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.  
The Supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect.  
The Supplier must propose an appropriate risk assessment and resolution plan.

4.3.2 Europe. 
a. Homologation and safety regulation activities.
The Supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.
   
b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s).
The supplier is responsible for getting their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests the Supplier to conduct tests as specified in certain of the UN-ECE at authorized technical service(s). The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.  
VinFast also requests that the Supplier provide supporting documentation and technical information, including drawings, to be submitted to the relevant authorities to get certification for the EC vehicle type-approval (if any).  
The Supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures that European authorities use to evaluate the compliance of their products with each corresponding UN-ECE regulation.  

c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations.
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced.  
The Supplier has a duty to notify VinFast about such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect.  
The Supplier must propose an appropriate risk assessment and resolution plan.

5.2 Details Deliverables and RASI.  
The RASI table outlines deliverables, roles, and responsibilities categorized as Responsible (R), Approval (A), Support (S), and Information (I). Mandatory updates to the RASI dates must align with the Master Timing from the PMO side.

The deliverable stages include:
- PV: List of Concept in Commodity and SOR for Concept in Commodity, with defined responsibilities and approvals at each step.
- TA: Feasibility for manufacturing and tooling, ensuring readiness for production.
- PR: Review stages for engineering changes and drawing finalization.
- CC: Final confirmation and DV completion prior to PV release.
- PTO: Readiness for tools and systems for production release.
- LS: Part appearance approval and final level sign-off.

For all stages, deliverables and assigned roles ensure systematic and consistent project execution. Clear approvals align with project milestones.

## 6 Supplier Responsibilities
Supplier is responsible to provide/ensure while completing the work. Supplier responsibilities are itemized to help mitigate the risk to the organization of an employer-employee relationship arising under the contract.

### Customer Service Requirements
#### After-Sales Spare Parts Should Be Ready at Startup
The spare parts provided by the supplier should be ready at the start of the whole vehicle production.

#### Spare Parts Support Cycle
The support period of after-sales spare parts shall not be shorter than 10 years after VinFast starts production of the whole vehicle, except as specified in this SOR.

#### Maintenance Information of After-Sale Spare Parts
The supplier of the first-level assembly should provide VinFast after-sales engineering department information about the design, use, and maintenance of the assembly before OTS, such as:
- BOM table
- Parts structure and function description
- Fault diagnosis
- Special tools for maintenance
- Relevant technical parameters (installation clearance, parts performance parameters, etc.)
