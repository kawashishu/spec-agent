3.7 Testing and validation and DVP&R.

**Test Plan**  
The test plan defines the responsibilities, timelines for testing, and required testing devices. It also presents the test outcomes visually and tracks testing progress. Test results must be controlled at all times.

The contracted entity for testing is B2P SUP.  
Test procedures and documentation need approval from VinFast's corresponding validation engineer and lead engineer.  

The test plan should be updated weekly or when new data/information arises due to development changes. VinFast should receive all relevant test protocols/reports and must be able to attend testing sessions, either at B2P SUP sites or their testing centers. Any deviation from approved procedures requires prior agreement between VinFast and B2P SUP.

If tests are conducted with incorrect parts, missing parts, or incorrect procedures, tests must be repeated at B2P SUP's expense. Negative results due to supplier fault also require a re-testing plan funded by B2P SUP.

**Included Data in DVP&R:**
- Testing with declaration of standard, norm, legal requirement.

Upon request, the results together with the documentation of the tests carried out (initial sample inspections and inspections carried out parallel to development) are to be submitted to the specialist department responsible for release. ### Test-report/-information:
- **Description/picture** of the part with documentation (part no., change index, materials, production process, additives like grease, and every deviation from approved serial process).
- **Labeling:** All parts must be labeled with a description.
- **Testing reason:** Eventually the reason for testing in case of e.g., negative test results and attachment of earlier test reports.
- **Testing standards:** Testing with exact naming of testing standard, norm, legal requirement, or exact description of agreed testing procedure.

### Documentation of result:
- **Result description:** Short description of the result (pictures, tables, detailed results, photo documentation, comparison with earlier test reports, evaluation of result OK/NOK, pictures of the parts in testing position, etc.).
- **Comparison:** Comparison with results of the specification book, testing specification, legal requirements, etc.
- **Recommendations:** Recommendation based on testing results.
- **Corrective measures:** Description of corrective measures or further approach in case of negative test results.
- **Testing status:** Progressional testing status.
- **Summary table:** Listing of all tests in a table with responsible person, part status, comparison of target date and due date. In case of negative test results, the B2P SUP has to provide a new date for re-test along with countermeasures for VF approval, including the responsible person and the company conducting the test.

### Test engineer notification:
The VF test engineer must be informed within 2 working days in the event of negative test results. Periodic intervals of updates regarding status and progress must also be communicated.

### Additional part testing:
If VinFast orders parts for testing independently, a detailed description of the ordered parts must be attached.

### Archival storage of tested parts:
Testing parts are only allowed to be scrapped if the VinFast test department agrees. Upon demand from the test engineer, the complete testing documentation (reports, parts, and descriptions) must be presented.

**3.8 Appearance Approval - Applicable for all Visible Parts**  
**AAR is a PPAP deliverable.**  
The AAR process is described in the VFDST00038401 (quantity of parts, timing).  

For each step of the process, Supplier has the responsibility to submit parts to VF Quality Design, following agreed timing with VF to get OK Shape, OKTG, and AAR Approval.

**Virtual Testing**  
Suitable calculation methods are to be used, and adequately detailed calculation models must be created by the B2P SUP to secure, verify, and effectively optimize the component properties defined in the technical specification, test procedures, and valid laws/regulations.  

Requirements related to the mechanical properties of the component—such as rigidity, strength, dimensional stability under heat, and passive safety—are to be secured using FEM calculations.  

The tool-dimensioning for the injection molding process is optimally guided by corresponding simulations. On request, operative calculation models representing the respective development status are to be made available at any time to VF technical departments for integration purposes.  

FEM models are to be provided without extra cost upon VF request. Tests must align with evaluation plans, and calculations are documented prior to tool dimensioning.  

**Component Testing**  
The detailed required testing will be defined within the DVP. The DVP must be agreed upon by VF, VinFast, and the B2P SUP to ensure continuity and accuracy.

**Mechanical Integration Tests**  
The following mechanical integration tests will be conducted on a complete vehicle:  
- **Climatic change test** (VFDST00046100)  
- **Sunlight simulation** (VFDST00053800)  
- **Mounting/dismounting** (VFDST00009000)  
- **Vibration combined with temperature** (VFDST00062600)  

**Key Requirements:**  
- No distortion or functional disturbance is permitted over the entire functional temperature range according to VTS.  
- Materials with high or varying thermal expansion coefficients are to maintain consistent behavior.  
- Flush and gap plan tolerances post-climate tests and sun simulation tests must not be exceeded.  

**Additional Notes:**  
Parts provided for testing activities at the VinFast test department are to be supplied free of charge by the B2P SUP. Detailed part descriptions are required, including tolerances and testing phases specified upon part delivery.

#### 4.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements

##### 4.3.1 North America

**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities**  
The supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations such as European standards, Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS).  
The supplier must provide and support any documentation to be submitted to the relevant authorities.

**b. Self-certification and test report(s)**  
The supplier is responsible for "self-certifying" their part(s), product(s), or system(s) to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before those products can be offered for sale.  
VinFast requests suppliers to conduct tests as specified in certain FMVSS/CMVSS. Test report(s) must be provided as evidence of compliance.  
The supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures (*) that USA/Canada governments use to evaluate compliance of their products with each FMVSS/CMVSS.  
(*) Refer to Appendix 8 of Manufacturer Handbook.

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS or European regulations**  
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a supplier may later determine non-compliance with FMVSS/CMVSS, European regulations, or a safety-related defect in a motor vehicle equipment item they produced. Suppliers must notify VinFast within five working days after determining a non-compliance or safety-related defect.
</page>

<page number="14">
The Supplier must propose appropriate risk assessment and resolution plan.

4.3.2 Europe.

a. Homologation and safety regulation activities.
The Supplier must ensure that their part(s), product(s) or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, e.g., United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) Regulations.

b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s).
The Supplier is responsible for ensuring that their part(s), product(s) or system(s) meet applicable UN-ECE regulations before those products can be offered for sale. VinFast requests the Supplier to conduct tests as specified in certain UN-ECE regulations at authorized technical service(s). The test report(s) and/or UN-ECE certificate(s) must be provided to VinFast as evidence of compliance.

VinFast also requests the Supplier to provide supporting documentation, technical information, including drawings to be submitted to the relevant authorities to get certification for the EC vehicle type-approval (if any).

The Supplier should also be familiar with the laboratory test procedures that European authorities use to evaluate the compliance of their products with each corresponding UN-ECE regulation.

c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations.
Notwithstanding its certification of a product, a Supplier may subsequently determine that a non-compliance with UN-ECE regulations or a safety-related defect exists in a motor vehicle equipment item it has produced. The Supplier has a duty to notify VinFast of such an issue within five working days after determining the existence of a non-compliance or a safety-related defect. The Supplier must propose an appropriate risk assessment and resolution plan.
