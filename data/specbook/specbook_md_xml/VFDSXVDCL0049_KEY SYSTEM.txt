### 5. Description and scope of work  

**5.1. Technical requirement**:  
The current size of the key + fob styling is **L 68mm x W39mm x H19mm**. The key must fit inside the key fob.  

#### Key blade with Plastic overmolding is required:  
- Key with overmolding must snap fit into the key fob and be held in place by a locking device.  
- Metal parts and systems must comply with anti-corrosion requirements for the whole vehicle and components.  
- The inserting and pulling force of the key must not exceed **14.7n**.  
- The rotation torque of the key must not exceed **0.28n·m**.  
- The key must remain functional after being stored at **-40°C ~ +90°C** for six hours.  
- Keys and locks exposed to the car body must withstand a **72-hour neutral salt spray test**.  
- Leakage must remain below **30ml/min at 5194Pa water pressure**.  
- Each set of keys must include at least **3,000 unique combinations**, supporting the inner tooth structure, anti-theft measures, and other functions.  
- The insertion and withdrawal force of the key must be rechecked following the testing requirements.

### Technical Requirements

**Key Blade with Plastic Overmolding Requirements:**
- Key with over molding must snap fit into the key fob and be held in place by a locking device.
- Metal parts and systems must meet anti-corrosion requirements for the whole vehicle and its parts.
- Inserting and pulling force of the key must not exceed 14.7 N.
- Rotation torque of the key must not exceed 0.28 N·m.
- The key must function intact after being stored at -40°C to +90°C for 6 hours.
- All locks exposed outside the vehicle body must withstand a 72-hour neutral salt spray test.
- Leakage amount must not exceed 30 ml/min at 5,194 Pa water pressure.
- Each key set must contain no fewer than 3,000 unique combinations while supporting anti-theft functions and the inner tooth structure of the key.
- Post-testing, the key's insertion and withdrawal forces must be evaluated.

**Durability Test and Compliance:**
- Door lock cylinders must endure a durability test of 40,000 uses.
- Suppliers must timely provide real IMDS data for parts and materials, which are prerequisites for PPAP (PSW) acceptance.
- Polypropylene is recommended as the plastic material.
- Substances such as lead, mercury, hexavalent chromium, polybrominated biphenyl ether, and polybrominated diphenyl ether cannot exceed 0.1% by mass, while cadmium must not exceed 0.01%. Exceptions apply only to exempted parts and materials.

**Regulatory and Appearance Requirements:**
- Suppliers must provide free test reports for lead, mercury, cadmium, and hexavalent chromium levels prior to PR.
- Finished products must not have sharp or rough edges.
- Key rotation must be smooth and clear without lag.
- Metal parts must comply with coating and treatment layer standards according to VFDST00007000 and VFDST00037500.

**Key Operation Requirements:**
- Key insertion and withdrawal must be flexible without lag. Between gears, the rotation torque must range from 0.15 N·m to 0.40 N·m, with START gear torque a minimum of 0.65 N·m.
- When inserted normally into a door lock cylinder, the key shouldn't deform under a 2.0 N·m torque applied for 10 seconds.
- Operating force for the door lock cylinder must have a rocker arm return spring torque not exceeding 0.28 N·m.
- The spring force with a door lock cylinder typically ranges from 1 N to 2 N.
- Key insertion/extraction force must not surpass 14.7 N.

**Compliance Standards Include:**

### Door Lock Cylinder Operating Force Requirements
The key's inserting and pulling force must not exceed 14.7 N. The rotation torque must be no greater than 0.28 N·m.

# Temperature resistance of the door lock cylinder.
The lock assembly can be operated and function intact after being stored at -40℃ ~ +90℃ for 6 hours.

# Corrosion resistance requirements of the door lock cylinder.
The key and all locks exposed to the outside of the car body shall withstand a 72-hour neutral salt spray test.

# Sealing performance requirements of the door lock cylinder.
Ensure that the leakage amount shall not exceed 30ml/min at the 5194Pa water pressure jet.

# Freezing resistance requirements of the door lock cylinder.
After freezing the door lock cylinder for at least 1.5 hours as required, check the insertion and withdrawal force of the key.

# Dust-proof performance requirements of the door lock cylinder.
Check the insertion and withdrawal force of the key after testing as required.

# Door lock cylinder durability requirements.
A durability test was carried out according to the test requirements, and the door lock cylinder was tested for 40,000 cycles.

# Door lock cylinder tooth combination requirements.
Each set of keys should contain no less than 3,000 different combinations that support the inner tooth structure of the key, anti-theft, and other related functions.

On request, operative calculation models representing development status must be made available for VINFAST technical departments for internal calculations and integration work.
