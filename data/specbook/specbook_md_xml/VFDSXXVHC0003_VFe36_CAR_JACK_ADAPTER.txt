### 4.3 Homologation and Safety Regulation Requirements.  

Safety regulations ensure supplier compliance with applicable standards and laws.  

#### 4.3.1 North America  

**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:**  
The supplier must ensure their part(s), product(s), or system(s) comply with applicable regulations, such as Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) and Canadian Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (CMVSS). Suppliers must provide documentation for submission to relevant authorities.  

**b. Self-certification and test report(s):**  
Suppliers are responsible for “self-certifying” their parts to meet all applicable FMVSS/CMVSS before products can be offered for sale. VinFast requires suppliers to conduct tests specified in FMVSS/CMVSS and provide test reports as evidence of compliance. Suppliers should understand laboratory test procedures used to evaluate compliance.  
*Refer to Appendix 8 of Manufacturer_Handbook.*  

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance:**  
Suppliers must notify VinFast within five working days of identifying any non-compliance or safety-related defect. A risk assessment and resolution plan must be proposed.  

#### 4.3.2 Europe  

**a. Homologation and safety regulation activities:**  
The supplier must ensure comprehensive compliance with European standards, such as United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UN-ECE) regulations.  

**b. Test report(s) and Certificate(s):**  
Suppliers must ensure their part(s) meet UN-ECE regulations, conduct tests at authorized technical services, and provide evidence (test reports or certificates). Supporting documentation and technical information should be submitted for EC type-approval certification. Suppliers must understand testing procedures used by European authorities.  

**c. Duty to notify VinFast of a non-compliance:**  
Suppliers must notify VinFast of identified non-compliance or safety defects within five working days, providing a risk assessment and resolution plan.
