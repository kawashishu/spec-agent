**3.2.1 Front Spring**

The front spring is a critical suspension component designed to optimize ride quality and stability.

Figure 1: Front Spring.

**3.2.2 Functional Data**

The following table outlines the spring data based on the final ride work results:

- The full bump length of the spring is 138.7 mm.
- The rebound length measures 272.7 mm.
- The installation length is specified as 198 mm.
- The free length of the spring is 330 mm.
- The spring motion ratio is calculated at 0.72.
- The spring rate is determined to be 64.7 N/mm.

3.6 Labeling of Parts  
Supplier must follow VinFast standard VFDST00000010. Suppliers should create figures indicating the position to stick labels on parts, using pictures to set up label positions accurately.  

3.7 Testing and validation and DVP&R  
DVP and PC tests must align with RASIC responsibilities. These include virtual and physical testing for systems, subsystems, and component levels, according to the Complete Vehicle Specification Book during the concept phase.  

Testing activities must adhere to the following:  
- Declaration of standard, norms, and legal requirements.  
- Details of the devices used for testing, including descriptions of the testing setup and pictures.  
- Identification of testing locations, whether internal or external.  
- Duration and project timeline for the testing.  
- Variants and detailed descriptions of the parts.  
- Number of tested components/parts.  

All testing should meet an aligned mileage target for a lifespan of 160,000 km or 10 years, whichever comes first. Further specifications can be found in the aligned DVP.  

3.8 Appearance Approval - Applicable for all Visible Parts  
AAR is a PPAP deliverable. The AAR process is elaborated in VFDST00038401, specifying the quantity of parts and timing requirements.  

The supplier must ensure coordination with VF Quality Design to receive approval for OK Shape, OKTG, and AAR within agreed timelines starting early at the PT build phase to secure AAR approval at LS build phase. This activity must be reflected in the supplier's development process and ED&D cost.

#### 5.1 Technical deliverables
Work Interface Chart (RASI). The main supplier obligations are described in the work interface chart (RASI). The status of the supplier ("System Development Supplier") is defined in System / Component Specification.

RASI is defined as follows:
- **R: Responsible**  
  Responsible for proper execution of the activities to fulfill the requirements. She/he is entitled to request the necessary support from other project team members.
- **A: Approval**  
  Obligation to decide / responsible for release. If no decider is defined, the person who is responsible for execution (R) is the decider.
- **S: Support**  
  Obligated to actively support (even if this requires capacity, expenses, and/or transfer of know-how). This project team member must proactively assist the person responsible for execution (R) in achieving the targets.
- **I: Information**  
  Right to information. This team member is entitled to demand information from (R).
