13.2.4 System Architecture.

Fully Automated Parking Assistance uses on-board sensors (USS and SVM camera) to identify the effective parking slot around the vehicle. The system controls the vehicle's moving, steering, shifting, and braking functions, enabling the vehicle to park into a parking slot or park out from a parking slot in a safe state. It is a driver assistance system that automatically controls a vehicle to park or exit a parking slot.

Fully Automated Parking Assistance includes both automatic parking in and automatic parking out functions.

Fully Automatic Parking Assist (FAPA): The parking slot and surrounding environment are perceived by the sensors around the vehicle. After the parking slot is determined, the system will carry out trajectory planning, and then the system will control the vehicle's moving, steering, gear shifting, and braking functions, so as to park the vehicle into the target parking slot within 7 times and the safest state.

Automatic parking system is divided into several sub-functions. Details are provided in the following table:

| System | Sub System  | Function Introduction  |
|--------|-------------|-------------------------|
| FAPA   | Parking In  | After entering the automatic parking function, the user drives the vehicle forward, and the system can identify the parking slot and judge the type of parking slot (parallel, perpendicular, and angled). After the target parking slot is determined, the user activates the automatic control of the vehicle, and then the vehicle can be parked smoothly in the target parking slot without the driver's operation. |
</page>

<page number="52">
parking out  
After entering the automatic parking function, the system will ask the driver to choose the direction and type of parking. After the preparation measures are completed, the user can activate the automatic control of the vehicle, and the vehicle will automatically and stably park out of the parking space (parallel, perpendicular, angled) without the driver's operation.

When the IDB fails (without losing braking ability), IDB sets its state to `ESC_ADAS_Available = 0x1: Not Available`. IDB automatically executes emergency braking, interrupts the handshake with FAPA, pulls up the EPB, and disconnects the handshake. The timing diagram illustrating these events is shown in 图6. At this point, FAPA requests the VCU to engage the P gear after the vehicle stops.

If the IDB fails and loses braking ability, iBooster must take the initiative to perform emergency braking. Once the vehicle stops, IDB/iBooster actively pulls up the EPB, and the handshake signal timing diagram for these events is depicted in 图6.

If the RCU fails, it sends a signal status: EPB Fault Status = `4 Temporary Fail` / `5 Permanent Fail`. In response, FAPA controls the IDB to perform an emergency stop. Once the vehicle stops, the P gear is engaged, and the disconnection handshake timing diagram (Figure 6) applies. After FAPA exits, IDB must maintain braking until the driver resumes control of the vehicle.

If FAPA requests the VCU to engage the P gear, but the VCU fails to engage within 2 seconds, IDB automatically pulls up the EPB.
